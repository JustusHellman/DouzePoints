The Legacy of the Eurovision Song Contest

The Eurovision Song Contest began in 1956 as a technical experiment in live, simultaneous, transnational broadcasting. Since then, it has grown into one of the world's most-watched non-sporting events, reaching hundreds of millions of viewers across the globe. It is a unique celebration of music, diversity, and international cooperation.

From the early days of orchestral ballads to the modern era of high-octane pop, rock, and experimental performance, Eurovision has always been a mirror of European culture and identity. It has launched the careers of global icons like ABBA and Celine Dion, and continues to be a platform for artistic innovation and cultural exchange every single year.

Our Daily Eurovision Games

  • EuroSong: Identify the hidden Eurovision song title in 6 attempts. A word-guessing game dedicated to the vast catalog of Eurovision song titles.
  • EuroArtist: Guess the name of the Eurovision artist or group in 6 tries. Challenges you to identify the famous performers and groups who have graced the stage.
  • EuroRefrain: Connect 16 words into four groups of four. Each group belongs to a different Eurovision song's refrain. Tests your memory for the most iconic lyrics.
  • EuroLinks: Group 16 Eurovision items into four categories of four based on a shared connection. A game of logic and Eurovision trivia.
  • EuroGuess: Identify a mystery Eurovision entry in 6 attempts using a series of clues like Year, Country, Genre, and Placing.
  • EuroArena: Compare stats to find the target entry. A data-driven guessing game where you use comparative statistics to find a hidden Eurovision entry.
